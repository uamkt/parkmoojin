  - question: "What is bleed air and why is it relevant to CBRN decontamination?"
    answer: "Bleed air is high-pressure, high-temperature air extracted from an aircraft's gas-turbine compressor before combustion. In conventional aerospace environmental control systems (ECS), it is conditioned through heat exchangers and expansion turbines to pressurize and thermally regulate cabin environments. Its relevance to CBRN decontamination lies in its inherent thermodynamic properties: compressor bleed stages routinely deliver air at 200–300 °C and 40–50 psi before conditioning. Applied to chemical agent neutralization, these temperature and pressure parameters exceed the thermal destruction thresholds of most Schedule 1 agents listed by the OPCW, including nerve agents such as Sarin (GB) and blister agents such as HD mustard.
